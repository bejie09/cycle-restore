Precise Step-by-Step Guide

Everything you need to know to get the most out of EnduraCore.

Setting Up Your FTP

Go to Today tab. Drag the Current FTP slider to match your most recent 20-min power × 0.95. Set Target FTP 15–30 W above current. This unlocks accurate Sweet Spot (88–94% FTP), Threshold (95–105% FTP), and VO2 Max (106–120% FTP) zones.

Logging Sleep Correctly

Each morning before riding, go to the Sleep tab. Enter last night's hours and rate your sleep quality (1–100). Upload a Garmin CSV/JSON export or photograph your smartwatch summary screen. The bedtime recommendation auto-adjusts to tonight's target based on today's planned training.

Uploading a Ride File

After completing a ride, run, or swim, go to the Training tab. Upload the .FIT, .TCX, .GPX, or .CSV file exported from Garmin Connect, Strava, or your head unit. AI extracts TSS, average power, and FTP estimate for rides, pace and heart rate for runs, or pace per 100m for swims. Alternatively tap 'Take / upload photo' and snap your Garmin screen — the AI reads the numbers from the image.

Reading Your Readiness Score

Score 82–100: Full training day — push threshold or FTP intervals. Score 65–81: Sweet spot or endurance ride, avoid spikes. Score 48–64: Recovery spin only, Zone 1–2 max. Score below 48: Rest day — sleep, food, and no structured work. The score recalculates instantly as you adjust inputs.

Logging Nutrition

Go to the Nutrition tab and set your daily carb, protein, fluid, and calorie targets — ask your AI Coach if you're not sure what to set them to. Then select Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner, or Other and either upload a food photo or tap 'Type manually' to describe your meal in plain English (e.g. '2 eggs, toast with peanut butter, banana, orange juice'). Each logged meal adds to today's total, and any nutrient still short of its target is flagged in red.

Chatting with Your Coach

Go to the Coach tab in the sidebar. Click 'Open AI Coach' — this opens your personal AI cycling coach in a new tab. Ask anything: interval structure, tapering strategy, nutrition for a 4-hour sportive, or why your FTP isn't climbing. The coach has full context about cycling performance science.
